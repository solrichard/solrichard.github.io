What would happen to the speed of water flowing out through a hose, if you were to cover part of the mouth oh the hose? The speed would increase, like obviously. Obvious? Maybe not.
This observation is given by a very simple explanation, that in fluid motion, the area of cross section of the source through which the fluid flows times the velocity of the fluid flowing is a constant.

That is, (Area) x (Velocity) = k, where k is a constant.

Consider water flowing through a pipe. The mass of water entering the pipe per unit time, must be equal to the amount of water flowing out.
=>  D x A1 x V1 = D x A2 x V2 (D=density, A=area,                                            V=velocity)
=>  A1 x V1 = A2 x V2
=>  (Area) x (Velocity) = k, where k is a constant.
This equation is also called Equation of Continuity.

That is, when one factor increases or decreases, the other should decrease or increase so as to keep A x V a constant.

This is the reason that when you cover the mouth of the hose, i.e., reduce the area, the velocity of water increases.
